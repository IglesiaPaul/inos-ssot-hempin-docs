Why Hemp First?

Hemp is not just a product category. It is a proof point for a different kind of economy.

The Challenge

The global hemp industry operates as a coordination failure at scale:

  • 57 jurisdictions with incompatible regulations
  • No shared identity standard for farmers, processors, brands
  • No machine-readable compliance layer
  • No verified counterparty graph

Why It's Hard (and Why That's Good)

The Environmental Imperative

Hemp is one of the most useful plants on earth:

  • 🌱 Carbon sequestration: 1 hectare of hemp absorbs 15 tonnes of CO₂
  • 💧 Water efficiency: 50% less water than cotton for equivalent fiber yield
  • ♻️ Circular potential: Every part of the plant can be used (fiber, seed, hurd, biomass)
  • 🌍 Soil regeneration: Phytoremediation capabilities clean contaminated land

If we can build verified truth infrastructure for hemp — the most fragmented, stigmatized, regulated plant-based industry — we can build it for anything.

Economic flows + 30% automatic routing to Regeneration Fund: Every transaction includes provenance trail linking fees to specific cleanup projects

The Regeneration Fund: 30% to Environmental Cleanup

Unlike traditional CSR or carbon offset programs, the Regeneration Fund is embedded in the architecture — not an add-on, not optional, not dependent on corporate goodwill.

How It Works

  1. Transaction occurs on HEMPIN (e.g., hemp fiber sale)
  2. 30% of value is automatically routed to Regeneration Fund (encoded in Value Layer of SSOT)
  3. Fund allocates to verified environmental cleanup projects (governed by community + expert panel)
  4. Impact is tracked and published transparently (provenance trail in graph)

Priority Project Types (Hemp-Specific)

🌱

Soil Remediation

Phytoremediation using hemp to clean heavy metal-contaminated land in Thailand. Restores degraded agricultural land; enables safe cultivation.

💧

Water Restoration

Wetland restoration in EU hemp-growing regions using hemp-based biofilters. Cleans contaminated waterways; protects freshwater ecosystems.

🌳

Carbon Sequestration

Hemp cultivation for carbon credits in regenerative farming systems. Sequesters 15 tonnes CO₂/hectare; generates verifiable carbon credits.

♻️

Waste Cleanup

Removal of agricultural plastic waste from hemp fields using hemp-based bioplastics. Reduces plastic pollution; creates circular economy loop.

🦋

Biodiversity Protection

Habitat restoration for pollinators in hemp-growing regions using hemp as companion crop. Supports ecosystem health; improves crop yields.

🔬

Farm Experiments

On-farm trials of regenerative practices: cover cropping, no-till, polycultures. Generates data for scalable soil health solutions.

Governance & Transparency

  • INOS Foundation (non-profit) holds the Regeneration Fund protocol — ensures mission lock
  • Expert panel (agronomists, ecologists, community reps) selects projects based on impact metrics
  • Public dashboard shows real-time fund contributions, project allocations, environmental impact
  • Community governance (long-term): token holders and stakeholders vote on fund allocation priorities
