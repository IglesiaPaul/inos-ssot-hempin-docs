# 1. Excellence

## 1.1 Long-term vision

**The Vision: Solarpunk 2045 — Verified Truth Infrastructure for Planetary Regeneration**

By 2045, global supply chains will operate on a layer of verified truth infrastructure that enables efficient cross-border trade while automatically enforcing regulatory compliance and funding environmental regeneration. This infrastructure will not be owned by any single corporation or state but will operate as a sovereign, open protocol — akin to TCP/IP for physical goods. The INOS/SSOT/HEMPIN project is the foundational research phase toward realizing this vision, starting with the hemp industry as a proof-of-concept domain.

**The Radically New Technology: Regulatory-Encoding Knowledge Graphs**

Current supply chain technologies (blockchain, ERP, traceability platforms) focus on recording transactions after they occur. They are backward-looking, siloed, and unable to encode complex regulatory logic across multiple jurisdictions. The radically new technology envisioned here is a **multi-dimensional knowledge graph with embedded regulatory science**.

This technology converges three distinct fields:
1.  **Graph Theory & Event Sourcing:** A single source of truth (SSOT) that encodes entities (farmers, processors, brands), relationships (trades, contracts), and value flows as immutable, time-series events.
2.  **Regulatory Science as Code:** Machine-readable encoding of 57+ jurisdictional regulations (THC limits, licensing, labeling) directly into the graph schema, enabling real-time compliance validation rather than post-hoc auditing.
3.  **AI-Native Onboarding (BUD Agent):** A natural-language-to-graph pipeline that allows non-technical actors (smallholder farmers) to verify onto the network via conversational AI, reducing onboarding friction from weeks to minutes.

This convergence creates a **Compliance Engine** that is proactive rather than reactive. Instead of discovering non-compliance at customs, the system prevents non-compliant transactions from being recorded in the first place. This is a paradigm shift from "trust but verify" to "verify by design."

**Long-Term Transformative Effects**

If achieved, this technology will have transformative positive effects on the economy, environment, and society beyond the project duration:

*   **Economy (Efficient Trade):** By reducing coordination failure in fragmented markets (starting with the $17B hemp industry), the technology enables efficient cross-border trade without manual compliance checks. Long-term, this reduces transaction costs by an estimated 30-40% for SMEs, unlocking access to global markets currently blocked by regulatory complexity. The technology scales beyond hemp to organic agriculture, textiles, and critical minerals by 2035.
*   **Environment (Regenerative Economics):** The architecture includes a **Regeneration Fund protocol** where 30% of transaction fees are automatically routed to verified environmental cleanup projects (soil remediation, water restoration). This embeds regeneration into the economic layer of supply chains, creating a sustainable funding mechanism for planetary repair independent of philanthropy or government grants.
*   **Society (Data Sovereignty):** By building on a sovereign EU technology stack (OVHcloud, Memgraph, Mistral AI), the infrastructure ensures data never leaves EU jurisdiction, reducing exposure to extraterritorial laws (e.g., US Cloud Act). This empowers European SMEs with data sovereignty, ensuring they control their commercial relationships rather than relying on US-based platforms.

**Project Contribution to the Vision**

The INOS/SSOT/HEMPIN project (48 months) is the **proof-of-principle research phase** (TRL 2-4) toward this 2045 vision. It does not aim to deploy the full global infrastructure but to validate the scientific and technological basis:

1.  **Validate Graph Schema:** Prove that regulatory rules can be encoded as graph constraints without losing flexibility for local variations (WP2, WP4).
2.  **Validate AI Onboarding:** Prove that natural language processing can accurately extract verified entities from unstructured farmer data with >85% confidence (WP3).
3.  **Validate Sovereign Stack:** Prove that EU-based infrastructure can handle the latency and throughput requirements for real-time compliance validation (WP2).
4.  **Validate Pilot Model:** Prove that actors will onboard to a verified network when compliance friction is removed (WP5, Thailand + EU pilots).

Successful completion of this project will de-risk the technology for subsequent Horizon Europe Innovation Actions (EIC Accelerator, Horizon Europe Cluster 4/6) and private investment, paving the way for full deployment by 2030. The hemp industry is the ideal testbed: high regulatory fragmentation, strong sustainability mandate, and fragmented SME landscape. Once proven in hemp, the architecture generalizes to other supply chains requiring verified trust.

**Alignment with EU Strategic Priorities**

This vision directly supports the EU's Strategic Autonomy (sovereign tech stack), Green Deal (regenerative funding), and Digital Decade (AI adoption in SMEs). It positions Europe as the leader in verified supply chain infrastructure, preventing dependency on US or Chinese platforms for critical trade data.

## 1.2 Science-towards-technology breakthrough

**The Core Breakthrough: Convergence of Graph Theory, Regulatory Science, and Sovereign AI**

The INOS/SSOT/HEMPIN project proposes a concrete science-towards-technology breakthrough: the development of a **Regulatory-Encoding Knowledge Graph** powered by a **Sovereign AI Onboarding Pipeline**. This is not an incremental improvement over existing supply chain technologies (blockchain, ERP, traceability platforms) but a fundamentally new architecture that shifts compliance from passive auditing to active prevention.

The breakthrough converges three distinct scientific domains that have not previously been integrated at this scale:
1.  **Natural-Language-to-Graph (NL2G) Pipeline:** Transforming unstructured conversational data into verified graph nodes with confidence scoring.
2.  **Machine-Readable Regulatory Ontology:** Encoding legal text (57 jurisdictions) into graph schema constraints that validate transactions in real-time.
3.  **Sovereign AI Infrastructure:** Self-hosted large language models (LLMs) on EU cloud infrastructure, eliminating dependency on US-based APIs for sensitive commercial data.

This convergence enables a **Compliance Engine** that operates at TRL 2-4 (proof-of-principle) during the project, paving the way for TRL 6+ deployment post-grant.

### 1.2.1 Concrete Breakthrough Components

**Breakthrough 1: BUD Agent (Natural-Language-to-Graph Pipeline)**

*   **State-of-the-Art (SotA):** Current supply chain onboarding requires manual form filling, document uploads, and weeks of verification by compliance officers. AI solutions exist but rely on US-based APIs (OpenAI, AWS) and lack graph integration, producing unstructured text outputs that cannot be verified.
*   **INOS/SSOT Innovation:** The BUD (Bot for Universal Data) Agent uses a fine-tuned Mistral 7B model to convert natural language conversations (e.g., farmer describing harvest) directly into structured graph nodes (Entity: Farmer, Relationship: Harvests, Attribute: 500kg, Compliance: THC<0.3%).
*   **Novelty:** Integration of **confidence scoring** (threshold >0.85) with **human-in-the-loop validation** for low-confidence extractions. This reduces onboarding friction from weeks to minutes while maintaining verification integrity.
*   **Advancement:** Moves AI from "chatbot" (TRL 6) to "verified data ingestion pipeline" (TRL 4). Requires research into hallucination mitigation for regulatory data (WP3).

**Breakthrough 2: Regulatory Ontology as Graph Constraints**

*   **State-of-the-Art (SotA):** Regulatory compliance is encoded as static PDFs or manual checklists. Verification occurs post-transaction (auditing), leading to costly rejections at customs or border controls. Blockchain solutions record transactions but cannot encode complex regulatory logic (e.g., "THC limits vary by jurisdiction and product type").
*   **INOS/SSOT Innovation:** Regulatory rules are encoded as **graph schema constraints** (e.g., Cypher queries, SHACL shapes). A transaction cannot be recorded if it violates a constraint (e.g., "Exporter without license cannot ship to Importer in Jurisdiction X").
*   **Novelty:** Active prevention vs. passive detection. The graph itself enforces compliance, reducing the need for external auditing.
*   **Advancement:** Moves regulatory tech from "document management" (TRL 5) to "executable compliance logic" (TRL 3-4). Requires research into ontology mapping across 57 jurisdictions (WP4).

**Breakthrough 3: Sovereign AI Stack (EU Data Sovereignty)**

*   **State-of-the-Art (SotA):** Most AI supply chain tools rely on US-based cloud providers (AWS, Azure, Google) and APIs (OpenAI). This exposes European SMEs to extraterritorial laws (US Cloud Act), creating data sovereignty risks for sensitive commercial relationships.
*   **INOS/SSOT Innovation:** Full stack sovereignty: OVHcloud (France) for hosting, Memgraph (Slovenia) for graph database, Mistral AI (France) for LLM inference. All infrastructure resides within EU jurisdiction.
*   **Novelty:** Demonstration that sovereign EU stack can handle latency/throughput requirements for real-time AI inference without US dependency. Requires GPU infrastructure for self-hosted LLMs (justifies €200K equipment cost in budget).
*   **Advancement:** Moves sovereign tech from "theoretical feasibility" (TRL 2) to "validated performance benchmark" (TRL 4). Critical for EU strategic autonomy (WP2).

**Overall Methodology**

*   **Models:** We will use **event-sourcing models** for immutable transaction history and **fine-tuned LLMs** (Mistral 7B) for natural language processing.
*   **Assumptions:**
    *   Assume regulatory text can be结构化 (structured) without losing legal validity (validated in WP4).

## 1.4 Interdisciplinarity

**The Interdisciplinary Challenge**

The INOS/SSOT/HEMPIN project addresses a problem that cannot be solved by a single discipline: enabling efficient cross-border trade in highly regulated supply chains. Current approaches are siloed: computer scientists build databases without encoding regulatory logic; legal scholars write regulations without considering machine-readability; agronomists optimize cultivation without digital verification layers. This siloed approach perpetuates the coordination failure we aim to solve.

To achieve the proposed breakthrough (Regulatory-Encoding Knowledge Graphs), the project converges **five distinct disciplines** that rarely collaborate at this depth:

1.  **Computer Science (Graph Theory & Event Sourcing):** Provides the architectural foundation for immutable, time-series data modeling (WP2).
2.  **Artificial Intelligence (NLP & LLMs):** Enables natural-language-to-graph conversion for low-friction onboarding (WP3).
3.  **Regulatory Science & Law:** Translates legal text (57 jurisdictions) into machine-readable graph constraints (WP4).
4.  **Agricultural Science (Agronomy):** Validates pilot data and ensures the graph reflects real-world cultivation practices (WP5).
5.  **Ecological Economics:** Designs the Regeneration Fund protocol to embed environmental cleanup into transaction layers (WP6).

**New Scientific Collaborations**

This project creates new collaborations between traditionally distant disciplines:

*   **AI Engineers + Regulatory Lawyers:** Typically, AI teams build models; legal teams review compliance post-deployment. In INOS/SSOT, they co-design the system: lawyers define the ontological constraints, engineers encode them as graph schemas (SHACL/Cypher). This ensures compliance is "baked in" rather than "audited on." This collaboration is novel because it treats regulation as a software specification, not a legal document.
*   **Graph Database Architects + Agronomists:** Typically, agronomists collect data in spreadsheets; database architects design schemas without field validation. In this project, agronomists (via prospective pilot partners) validate the graph schema against real-world hemp cultivation data (e.g., harvest cycles, THC testing protocols). This ensures the SSOT reflects biological reality, not just digital abstraction.
*   **Economists + Software Developers:** Typically, economists model markets; developers build transaction systems. Here, economists design the Regeneration Fund protocol (30% fee allocation), and developers implement it as smart-contract-like logic within the graph. This embeds economic policy directly into the infrastructure layer.

**Contribution to the Proposed Breakthrough**

The interdisciplinary approach is not optional; it is the core mechanism enabling the breakthrough:

*   **Without Regulatory Science:** The graph would be a generic database, unable to prevent non-compliant transactions. The "verify-by-design" paradigm would fail.
*   **Without AI/NLP:** Onboarding would require manual form filling, perpetuating the friction we aim to eliminate. The BUD Agent would not exist.
*   **Without Agronomy:** The schema would be theoretically sound but practically unusable by farmers. Pilot validation would fail.
*   **Without Ecological Economics:** The system would optimize trade but not regeneration. The Solarpunk 2045 vision would be incomplete.

**Evidence of Interdisciplinary Integration**

The work plan demonstrates deep integration, not parallel workstreams:

*   **WP2 (Architecture) + WP4 (Compliance):** Graph schema design (CS) is iterative with regulatory ontology encoding (Law). You cannot finalize the schema without knowing the regulatory constraints.
*   **WP3 (AI) + WP5 (Pilots):** BUD Agent training (AI) uses real farmer conversation data (Agronomy). You cannot fine-tune the model without field data.
*   **WP6 (Dissemination) + All WPs:** Open science practices ensure all disciplines contribute to publications (e.g., joint papers on "AI for Regulatory Compliance" or "Graph Databases for Agronomic Traceability").

**Conclusion**

The INOS/SSOT/HEMPIN project is inherently interdisciplinary. The breakthrough (Regulatory-Encoding Knowledge Graphs) exists at the intersection of CS, AI, Law, Agronomy, and Economics. No single discipline could achieve this alone. By forcing these disciplines to collaborate at the schema design level (not just the integration level), the project creates a new scientific paradigm: **compliance as code, verified by design.** This is the essence of the interdisciplinary innovation proposed here.
